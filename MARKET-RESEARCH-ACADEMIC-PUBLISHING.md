## 1. INDIAN ACADEMIC PUBLISHING MARKET

### 1.1 Scale and Volume

India is the **3rd largest producer of research papers globally** (after China and the US).

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Annual research papers | ~275,000+ (2022, Scopus) |
| Research output growth (2017-2022) | **54%** |
| Higher education institutions | 58,000+ (1,113 universities, 43,796 colleges) |
| Total student enrollment | 43.3 million |
| Nature Index ranking (2025) | **9th globally** |
| Global share of publications | 8.05% |
| Indian journals in Scopus | 533 |

India's research output grew **54% from 2017 to 2022**, making it one of the fastest-growing research nations. The government-backed One Nation One Subscription (ONOS) initiative, budgeted at ~$715 million over 3 years, is expected to increase beneficiaries from 6.5 million to 18.7 million researchers.

### 1.2 What Indian Researchers Search For

Based on Google search patterns and journal websites targeting Indian researchers, the most common search queries include:

- "UGC CARE approved journals list 2025/2026"
- "Scopus indexed journals with low publication charges"
- "fast publication journals India"
- "call for papers 2025/2026"
- "publish research paper online"
- "journal for publication with low APC"
- "international journal for paper publication"
- "UGC approved journal for PhD"
- "best journal to publish paper"
- "free publication journals"

**Key insight for Google Ads**: Indian researchers are extremely price-conscious and frequently include cost-related terms ("low charges," "free," "low APC") in their searches. They also strongly prioritize indexing status (Scopus, UGC CARE).

### 1.3 Most Popular Academic Platforms in India

| Platform | Usage | Role |
|----------|-------|------|
| **Google Scholar** | Dominant search tool | Primary discovery, citation tracking |
| **ResearchGate** | 79.5% satisfaction rate among users | Networking, paper sharing, profile building |
| **Scopus** | Used via institutional access | Indexing verification, impact metrics |
| **Academia.edu** | Widely used | Paper sharing, discoverability |
| **Web of Science** | Used via institutional access | Citation tracking, journal ranking |
| **PubMed** | Medical/health researchers | Biomedical literature search |

92.6% of Indian researchers using ResearchGate reported being at least "satisfied" with the platform. Google Scholar is used roughly twice as often as ResearchGate for actual search functionality.

### 1.4 University Publishing Requirements

Indian universities have **strict publishing requirements** tied to:

- **PhD submission**: Most universities require 1-3 publications in recognized journals before thesis defense
- **Faculty promotions (CAS)**: Career Advancement Scheme mandates publications in recognized journals
- **API (Academic Performance Indicator) scores**: Publications carry significant weight in faculty evaluations
- **Hiring**: Elite institutions require publications in high-impact international journals

### 1.5 UGC CARE List - What It Is and Why It Matters

**UGC-CARE** = Consortium for Academic and Research Ethics, established by the University Grants Commission in 2018.

**Purpose**: Curates a list of trusted, peer-reviewed journals to promote academic integrity and prevent predatory publishing.

**Journal Groups**:
- **Group A**: Journals indexed in Scopus or Web of Science
- **Group B**: Previously listed journals that passed quality analysis
- **Group C**: Journals recommended by UGC-CARE Council members
- **Group D**: Journals submitted by universities that passed analysis

**Why it matters for AIR**:
- Indian universities **ONLY recognize publications from UGC-CARE listed journals** for promotions, PhD submissions, and evaluations
- Getting on the UGC-CARE list would instantly make AIR relevant to ~275,000+ annual Indian researchers
- Without CARE listing, many Indian researchers cannot justify publishing with AIR regardless of quality
- **Pathway**: Getting indexed in Scopus or Web of Science automatically qualifies for Group A

### 1.6 Why Indian Researchers Choose International Journals

Key motivators (from surveys and research):

1. **International recognition and prestige** - Indian value system gives more weight to papers published overseas
2. **Wider readership** - International journals have larger, more diverse audiences
3. **Disciplinary gaps** - Many specialist fields lack quality Indian journals
4. **Career advancement** - Elite Indian institutions prefer hiring faculty with international publications
5. **Quality of peer review** - Indian journal peer review often perceived as lower standard
6. **Indexing** - International journals more likely to be Scopus/WoS indexed
7. **Institutional bias** - The "scientific establishment does not have strong faith in the quality of research output from the majority of laboratories in India"

### 1.7 Price Sensitivity and APC Ranges

Indian researchers are **extremely price-sensitive**:

- Most Indian institutes do **not pay for publication or research costs**
- India does **not qualify** for APC waivers from most major publishers (it's classified as a middle-income country)
- Limited funding opportunities prevent less affluent scholars from publishing
- Government's ONOS scheme now covers some APCs for top-tier journals

**Acceptable APC ranges for Indian researchers**:

| Category | APC Range | Researcher Reaction |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Predatory/low-quality | $50-200 (Rs 4,000-16,000) | Readily affordable, widely used |
| Budget legitimate | $200-500 (Rs 16,000-40,000) | Sweet spot for self-funded researchers |
| Mid-range legitimate | $500-1,000 | Possible with departmental support |
| High-end OA | $1,000-3,000+ | Requires grant funding, prohibitive for most |

**AIR opportunity**: An APC of **$200-400** would be competitive and accessible to a large portion of Indian researchers while being 2-3x above predatory pricing, signaling legitimacy.

### 1.8 Top Disciplines Publishing from India

| Discipline | Share of Output | Growth Rate |
|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| Computer & Information Sciences | 21% | 13.91% (fastest) |
| Engineering | 17% | 12.83% |
| Biological & Biomedical Sciences | 13% | - |
| Environmental Sciences | - | 13.43% |
| Chemistry & Materials Science | Major field | - |
| Medical & Health Sciences | Major field | - |
| Social Sciences | 5% | - |

**Key takeaway**: Engineering, CS, and health sciences dominate Indian research output. Social sciences and humanities are underrepresented but growing, creating an opportunity for a multidisciplinary journal.

## 2. CHINESE ACADEMIC PUBLISHING MARKET

### 2.1 Volume and Trends

China is the **#1 research-producing country in the world**.

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Papers in Web of Science (2024) | **878,300** |
| Annual growth rate | 11.3% |
| Nature Index articles (2024) | 37,273 (vs US: 31,930) |
| Nature Index growth (2020-2024) | **95%** |
| Global share of top 1% cited papers | Roughly at parity with the US |
| Total Scopus publications | ~1,216,000 |

China grew its Nature Index output by 95% from 2020 to 2024. In chemistry, Earth/environmental sciences, and physical sciences, China leads globally.

### 2.2 Google and VPN Situation

**Google is blocked** behind China's Great Firewall. However:

- **30-50 million mainland users** access Google via VPN or roaming e-SIM
- VPN usage in mainland China **nearly doubled year-on-year** in 2024
- The heaviest VPN users are **younger, tech-savvy, educated** people - exactly the researcher demographic
- Researchers routinely use VPNs to access Google Scholar, international journals, and academic resources
- Universities often provide institutional VPN access for research purposes

**Can Google Ads reach Chinese researchers?** Partially, yes:
- Google Ads are "invisible behind China's Great Firewall" for most users
- But VPN-using researchers DO see Google Ads
- The reach is limited (estimated 30-50 million VPN users out of 1+ billion population)
- **Alternative**: Baidu Ads (Baidu PPC) would reach the majority of Chinese researchers

### 2.3 Platforms Chinese Researchers Use

| Platform | Role | Reach |
|----------|------|-------|
| **CNKI (China National Knowledge Infrastructure)** | Primary academic database | Most universities provide free access |
| **Baidu Scholar** | Academic search engine (like Google Scholar) | Integrated into Baidu ecosystem, massive reach |
| **Wanfang Data** | Academic database | Widely used for Chinese-language publications |
| **CSSCI** | Chinese Social Sciences Citation Index | Standard for social science journal ranking |
| **Google Scholar** | Used via VPN | Preferred for international literature |
| **Web of Science / Scopus** | Via institutional access | Used for journal ranking/metrics |
| **WeChat** | Social/academic communication | 1+ billion monthly users |
| **Zhihu** | Q&A platform (like Quora) | Expert-led discussions, high search intent |

### 2.4 WeChat and Zhihu - Should AIR Have Presence?

**WeChat**:
- **65.3% of CSSCI-indexed journals** have created WeChat public accounts
- Over **193,000 WeChat posts** from academic journals total
- Publishers use WeChat to "build brand awareness, engage with researchers, and drive journal submissions"
- Major Western publishers (Nature, Elsevier, Wiley) have WeChat accounts
- **Recommendation**: YES, a WeChat public account would be valuable for reaching Chinese researchers

**Zhihu**:
- Expert-led Q&A platform with high credibility
- Users have "high search intent for well-informed content"
- Cross-platform strategy: Zhihu visibility drives users to WeChat for direct engagement
- **Recommendation**: Secondary priority - useful for establishing thought leadership

### 2.5 What Chinese Researchers Value Most in a Journal

From surveys of Chinese researchers (2021):

1. **Journal reputation** - #1 factor
2. **Journal impact metrics (Impact Factor)** - Critical for career evaluation
3. **Publisher brand prestige** - CNS (Cell/Nature/Science) branded journals = gold standard
4. **Field-specific peer recognition** - Highly valued
5. **Indexing in SCI/SSCI** - Essential for career promotion and funding

Chinese researchers have a **higher preference for Impact Factor** compared to US/UK researchers, driven by how Chinese institutions evaluate researchers for funding and career advancement.

**Key insight**: Chinese researchers will be attracted to a journal that has (or is pursuing) Scopus/WoS indexing and can demonstrate growing citation metrics. The path to Impact Factor is the key selling point.

## 3. BRAZILIAN ACADEMIC PUBLISHING MARKET

### 3.1 Scale and Growth

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Global ranking (2013-2018) | 13th by research productivity |
| Publications during 2013-2018 | 250,680 |
| Annual publications (2018) | 50,000+ |
| Growth rate (2013-2018) | 30% (twice the global mean) |
| SciELO Brazil journals | 300+ |
| SciELO cumulative documents | 500,000+ |
| New SciELO documents per year | ~22,000 |

### 3.2 SciELO Platform and Its Role

**SciELO (Scientific Electronic Library Online)** is Brazil's flagship open-access platform:

- Over **300 open-access journals** across disciplines
- Cumulative repository of **500,000+ documents**
- Updated with ~22,000 new documents annually
- Over 180 academic institutions own the non-profit journals
- Indexed in Web of Science (SciELO Citation Index)
- **Free for authors** - no APCs for most SciELO journals

SciELO serves as both a hosting platform and a quality filter. Being indexed in SciELO carries significant prestige within Latin America.

### 3.3 CAPES Requirements for PhD Students

**CAPES** (Coordenadoria de Aperfeicoamento de Pessoal de Nivel Superior) is a government agency under the Brazilian Ministry of Education.

**QUALIS System**:
- Classifies journals into tiers: **A1, A2, B1, B2, B3, B4, B5, C**
- Updated every 4 years
- Based on: number of issues, indexing, impact factor (JCR), number of publishing institutions
- CAPES evaluates master's and doctoral programs based on the quality of journals in which research is published

**Impact on researchers**:
- PhD programs are evaluated on a **3-7 scale** by CAPES
- Programs rated 6-7 receive more funding
- Publishing in higher-QUALIS journals directly improves program ratings
- Projects in QUALIS-ranked journals are more likely to receive funding
- **Bottom line**: Brazilian researchers MUST publish in QUALIS-recognized journals for career advancement

**AIR opportunity**: Getting classified in QUALIS (even at B-level initially) would open the door to Brazilian submissions.

### 3.4 How to Reach Brazilian Researchers via Google Ads

- Google is the **dominant search engine** in Brazil (no firewall issues)
- Portuguese-language ads would perform better for initial awareness
- Key search terms to target:
  - "publicar artigo cientifico" (publish scientific article)
  - "revista cientifica internacional" (international scientific journal)
  - "journal for publication"
  - "open access journal low APC"
  - "Qualis A journal call for papers"
- **Springer Nature + CAPES Transformative Agreement** (2024) signals growing OA adoption
- Brazil is a **Tier 2 CPC market** - Google Ads are cheaper than US/UK

### 3.5 Language Considerations

- Most Brazilian researchers can read English at academic level
- However, many prefer to write initially in Portuguese
- SciELO journals are increasingly bilingual
- **Strategy**: Accept English-only submissions but offer Portuguese-language marketing materials and website content
- A Portuguese landing page could significantly improve conversion rates

### 3.6 Top Disciplines from Brazil

| Discipline | Annual Papers | Top 1% Share |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Natural Sciences | 2,200 | 0.95% |
| Medical Sciences | 1,415 | - |
| Agricultural Sciences | 783 | 0.67% |
| Engineering & Technology | 676 | 0.72% |
| Social Sciences | 576 | 0.98% |
| Humanities | 118 | 0.68% |

Strengths: **Dentistry** (world-leading), tropical medicine, STEM (batteries, solar cells, nanotechnology), agriculture, environmental sciences, women's health care.

Health and biological sciences produced **44.2% of all Brazilian articles** in Science Citation Index databases historically.

## 4. OTHER KEY MARKETS

### 4.1 South Korea

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Publication culture | Extremely metrics-driven |
| Domestic index | KCI (Korea Citation Index), run by NRF |
| Preferred international indices | SCI/SCIE, SSCI, Scopus |
| Professor output (applied sciences) | 10.5 SCI-level articles/year per capita |
| Professor output (pure sciences) | 7.5 articles/year |
| Professor output (social sciences) | 1.46 articles/year |
| Professor output (humanities) | 0.68 articles/year |

**Key dynamics**:
- Publishing in SCI/SCIE journals earns **2-2.4x more credit** than KCI journals
- SSCI credit is **more than 2x** domestic journal credit
- Professors receive **monetary bonuses** for each article (reduced by number of coauthors)
- Prestigious private universities differentiate credit by **Impact Factor quartiles**
- South Korean researchers are motivated by both career advancement AND direct financial incentives

**Targeting strategy**: Emphasize international indexing trajectory, competitive turnaround times. Korean researchers actively search for English-language OA journals.

### 4.2 Nigeria

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Contribution to predatory journals | 3.3% (64 articles in sample study, vs India's 93%) |
| Key challenge | Funding, institutional support, APCs |
| APC barrier | 87.6% of academic librarians cited APCs as a barrier |
| Main driver of predatory publishing | Promotion pressure + lack of access to quality journals |

**Key dynamics**:
- "Publish or perish" pressure is intense for faculty promotions
- Institutional support and funding for research are severely limited
- Nigerian researchers "have plenty of clinical material and potential" but lack resources
- Early-career academics are most vulnerable to predatory publishers
- Free or very low-cost journals are the primary draw

**Targeting strategy**: APC waivers or significant discounts for Nigerian authors could attract quality submissions while building a diverse author base. Emphasize legitimate peer review as an alternative to predatory options.

### 4.3 Iran

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Growth | Replaced Taiwan in top 15 S&E article producers (2014) |
| Health sciences growth | 250%+ increase (2010-2022) |
| Access crisis (2024) | 82% of universities lost access for 1+ week in Q2 2024 |
| Partial access | Only 12% of universities maintained partial access |
| IEEE access decline | 72% decline in conference paper access over 6 months |

**Unique challenges**:
- International sanctions restrict journal database access
- Payment mechanisms are unreliable due to banking sanctions
- Researchers rely on VPNs, pirated content, and international collaborations
- The cost of accessing journals is "prohibitive due to high exchange rate"
- Iran's government has been preparing to further wall off the internet

**Targeting strategy**:
- Google Ads CAN reach Iranian researchers (most use VPNs)
- APC payment is the biggest barrier - would need alternative payment methods
- Iranian researchers are highly motivated to publish internationally but face massive structural barriers
- A journal that offers APC waivers and easy payment would attract high volumes
- Risk: US sanctions compliance must be carefully evaluated

### 4.4 Turkey

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Journals in WoS + Scopus | 120 |
| Historical challenge | "Low productivity for many years" |
| Research growth driver | University expansion in the 2000s-2010s |

Turkey has significantly expanded its university system and research output in recent decades, though quality metrics have lagged. Turkish researchers actively publish in international journals, particularly in engineering, medical sciences, and social sciences.

### 4.5 Southeast Asia

**Indonesia**:
- Went from **2nd worst to top producer** of scientific articles in Southeast Asia in 3 years
- Published **50,868 Scopus papers in 2020** (up from much lower levels)
- Government set ambitious publication targets in the 2020-2024 development plan
- Engineering predominates among publications
- Fastest-growing research market in ASEAN

**Philippines**:
- Total scholarly output: **5,291** (significantly behind regional peers)
- Behind Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, and Singapore in publications and citations
- Government has been slow to implement publishing incentive policies
- Potential growth market if policies change

**Vietnam**:
- Rapidly growing research output
- Engineering is the dominant field
- Part of the broader ASEAN publishing growth trend

**Regional strategy**: Indonesia is the standout opportunity in Southeast Asia - large researcher population, government-backed push for international publications, growing budget for research.

## 5. UNIVERSITY PROFESSORS / ESTABLISHED RESEARCHERS AS TARGETS

### 5.1 "Publish or Perish" - Who Is Most Affected?

**Most affected groups** (in order):

1. **Junior/tenure-track faculty** - "Tremendous, sometimes crippling, pressure to publish in peer-reviewed journals for junior faculty, but the pressure goes away for senior faculty"
2. **PhD candidates** - Must publish to complete degree requirements in many countries
3. **Post-doctoral researchers** - Publications determine future employment prospects
4. **Mid-career faculty seeking full professorship** - Need continued publication record
5. **Faculty in developing countries** - Promotion tied directly to publication counts

**US tenure landscape**: Tenured faculty dropped from **39% (1987) to 24% (2021)**, while contingent appointments rose from 47% to 68%. This means more researchers are in precarious positions requiring continuous publishing.

**Most affected countries by publish-or-perish pressure**:
- **India**: API scores, CAS promotions
- **China**: Funding and promotion tied to SCI publications
- **South Korea**: Monetary bonuses per paper, promotion credits
- **Nigeria**: Promotion requires publications, funding is scarce
- **Brazil**: QUALIS classification affects program funding
- **Iran**: Career advancement depends on international publications despite access barriers

### 5.2 Google Ads Targeting Strategies for Researchers

**Can Google Ads target university email domains?**
- Not directly by email domain in Search campaigns
- **BUT**: Google Ads **Customer Match** allows uploading email lists to create custom audiences
- You can upload lists of .edu/.ac email addresses to target specific researchers
- Minimum list size: **100 members** (added/updated within 540 days)
- Customer Match works across: Search, Gmail, YouTube, Display Network

**Custom audience creation methods**:

| Method | How It Works | Effectiveness |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Customer Match (email lists)** | Upload researcher email lists | High - direct targeting |
| **Custom Intent Audiences** | Target people searching specific keywords | High for active researchers |
| **In-Market Audiences** | Target people "in market" for education/research | Medium |
| **Affinity Audiences** | Target by interests (science, research, academia) | Low-medium |
| **Website/URL targeting** | Target visitors to academic websites | High for Display/YouTube |
| **Similar Audiences** | Expand from existing customer lists | Medium |

### 5.3 Using Author Databases for Targeting

**Available databases for building email lists**:

1. **ORCID** - Open database of researcher identifiers, but emails are often private
2. **ResearchGate** - Profiles with contact info (scraping violates ToS)
3. **Conference proceedings** - Author emails typically published
4. **Journal articles** - Corresponding author emails are published
5. **University faculty directories** - Publicly available
6. **LinkedIn** - Can target by job title ("professor," "researcher," "PhD candidate")

**Practical approach for AIR**:
- Build email lists from published corresponding authors in target disciplines
- Use conference attendee lists (many conferences sell or share attendee data)
- Scrape university faculty pages (legal if publicly available)
- Upload these lists to Google Ads Customer Match and Facebook Custom Audiences
- Create "lookalike" audiences based on these seed lists

### 5.4 Academic Conference Attendee Targeting

- Conference websites can be used as URLs for Google Custom Audiences
- Target people who visited specific conference websites
- Many conferences have sponsor/exhibitor packages that include attendee data
- **LinkedIn**: Can target by conference attendance, professional groups, university affiliation

### 5.5 Department Mailing Lists as a Channel

- University department mailing lists are **not typically available** to external marketers
- However, department **contact pages** and **faculty directories** are public
- Direct email outreach to department heads/chairs can be effective
- **Alternative**: Partner with academic societies that maintain member mailing lists
- Some academic associations sell advertising in newsletters

## 6. PREDATORY VS LEGITIMATE JOURNAL CHOICE

### 6.1 Why Researchers Choose Predatory Journals

| Reason | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Speed** | Publication in 2-3 months vs 6-18 months for legitimate journals |
| **Guaranteed acceptance** | No real peer review = no rejection |
| **Low cost** | Average APC of $178 vs $937-1,221 for legitimate OA |
| **Career pressure** | "Publish or perish" drives desperation |
| **Lack of awareness** | Early-career researchers don't know how to identify predatory journals |
| **Language barriers** | Easier acceptance for non-native English speakers |
| **Geographic isolation** | Researchers in developing countries have limited access to quality journals |

### 6.2 What Makes Researchers Switch to Legitimate Journals

1. **Reputational damage** - Discovering their publications "don't count"
2. **Institutional warnings** - Universities issuing lists of predatory journals
3. **Career consequences** - Promotion committees rejecting predatory publications
4. **UGC CARE / QUALIS requirements** - Only approved journals count
5. **Peer judgment** - Colleagues recognizing predatory publications
6. **Retraction risks** - Work in predatory journals has no archiving guarantees

### 6.3 Price Comparison

| Category | APC Range | Peer Review | Time to Publication | Indexing |
|----------|-----------|-------------|--------------------|---------|
| **Predatory** | $50-200 (avg $178) | None/fake | 1-4 weeks | None/fake |
| **Low-end legitimate OA** | $200-500 | Real but basic | 2-4 months | Some indexing |
| **Mid-range legitimate OA** | $500-1,500 | Rigorous | 3-6 months | Scopus/WoS |
| **High-end legitimate OA** | $1,500-4,000+ | Top-tier | 4-12 months | High IF journals |
| **Average legitimate OA** | $937 per article | Yes | Variable | Variable |

### 6.4 Can a Legitimate Journal Compete on Price While Maintaining Quality?

**YES, but with caveats**:

**Sustainable low-APC model requirements**:
- **Volunteer editorial board** (common in early-stage journals)
- **Minimal staff** (1-2 managing editors)
- **Open-source publishing platform** (OJS - Open Journal Systems is free)
- **No print edition** (online-only)
- **Lean operations** (no fancy office, low overhead)

**Viable APC pricing for AIR**:

| Target Market | Suggested APC | Rationale |
|--------------|--------------|-----------|
| Standard rate | $350-500 | Above predatory ($178 avg), below mid-range ($937 avg) |
| Developing country discount | $150-250 | Competitive with predatory on price, legitimate on quality |
| Full waiver (LDCs) | $0 | For least-developed countries, builds diversity |

**Key differentiators vs predatory journals (at similar price point)**:
- Real peer review (even if basic - single-blind with 2 reviewers)
- DOI assignment for every article
- Proper indexing (CrossRef, Google Scholar, DOAJ)
- Transparent editorial process
- Named, verifiable editorial board
- Consistent publication schedule
- Retraction policies
- ISSN
- Path to Scopus/WoS indexing

### 7.4 Pricing Strategy

**Recommended APC structure**:

|------|-----------|-----|
| Tier 1 (High income) | US, UK, EU, Australia, Japan, S. Korea | $495 |
